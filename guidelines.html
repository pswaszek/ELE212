ELE 212 - Guidelines for Problem Submission

Proper technical communication of engineering work is essential for success. As an engineer, submitting sloppy, incomplete, and/or disorganized work is a quick way to damage your image and can lead to inaccurate and unsafe designs; it is therefore important for students to develop good communication habits early in their studies. While not everyone can draw like Michelangelo or letter like a 15-th century monk, we all can create handwritten solutions that are organized and neat. Toward that end, the following specifications are hereby established for work submitted in this class:

  1. Use standard size 8-1/2" x 11" paper for submitted work; do not use sheets with ragged edges torn from notebooks or recycled paper from another course or someone's old company letterhead.
  2. Start each problem on a new page; print your name and the problem number at the top.
  3. Problem solutions should normally contain a sketch of the object under study, e.g. the circuit. Appropriate quantities should be labeled on the sketch.
  4. Start your solutions from the fundamental laws/concepts, then specialize the general relations for the problem under study, substituting in for the variables that are pertinent to the particular problem.
  5. All major steps, including substitutions and algebraic simplifications, should be clearly understandable. Don't be afraid to add a few words about how you did things.
  6. While calculators give vast numbers of digits for answers, think about the number of digits that are sufficient and round off appropriately.
  7. Final answers should include appropriate units and be highlighted in an enclosed box or underlined.
College instructors normally encourage students to form study groups, and this can lead to collaborative solutions. For such cases, however, you should independently re-develop your own solution. Directly copying another student's work is a form of cheating and can lead to serious consequences as outlined in the URI Student Handbook.
